SECTION: Domestic violence termination

Since 2019, a tenant who is a victim of domestic violence can terminate their tenancy immediately and without penalty by giving the landlord a Domestic Violence Termination Notice (s105B - s105E RTA 2010) accompanied by one of: a final apprehended domestic violence order against the perpetrator; a court order, conviction, or charge for a relevant offence; or a declaration in the prescribed form by a qualified medical practitioner.

Co-tenants who are not victims continue to be liable for their share of the rent. The landlord cannot list the departing tenant on a residential tenancy database (rental blacklist) for any debt arising from the early termination. Bond claims continue under the standard process via NSW Fair Trading.
