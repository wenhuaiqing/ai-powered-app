SECTION: Rent increases

A landlord may increase rent in a periodic agreement no more than once every 12 months and must give the tenant at least 60 days written notice specifying the new amount and effective date. In a fixed-term agreement of less than 2 years, rent cannot be increased unless the agreement states the new amount or the calculation method.

Tenants can challenge an excessive rent increase by applying to NCAT within 30 days of receiving notice. NCAT will compare the new rent to market rates for comparable premises in the area and may order a lower increase or no increase.
