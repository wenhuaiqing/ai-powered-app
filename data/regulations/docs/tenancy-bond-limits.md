SECTION: Rental bonds

The maximum bond a landlord can request in NSW is four weeks' rent for an ongoing residential tenancy, regardless of whether the property is furnished or unfurnished. Holiday lets and boarding houses are excluded.

All bonds must be lodged with NSW Fair Trading via Rental Bonds Online (RBO) within 10 working days of receipt. Landlords or agents who fail to lodge can be fined up to $2,200. A tenant can apply to the NSW Civil and Administrative Tribunal (NCAT) to recover an unlodged bond.

Claims on the bond at the end of a tenancy are made through RBO. If the parties agree on the split, the bond is refunded within two business days. If there is a dispute, either party can apply to NCAT within three months of the tenancy end.
