SECTION: Residential tenancy databases (blacklists)

A landlord or agent may only list a former tenant on a residential tenancy database (e.g. TICA, NTD) if all three conditions are met: the tenant's tenancy has ended; the tenant has been notified in writing of the proposed listing and given at least 14 days to respond; and the listing is for a permitted reason — typically rent arrears exceeding the bond, or a Tribunal order for breach.

Listings must be removed when the listed amount is paid in full, or after 3 years, whichever is earlier. Tenants have the right to know if they are listed (request copy within 14 days, no fee from the operator) and to apply to NCAT for an order requiring inaccurate or unjust listings to be removed.
