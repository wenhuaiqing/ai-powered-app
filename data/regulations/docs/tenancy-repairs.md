SECTION: Repairs and maintenance

Landlords must ensure the premises are reasonably clean, fit for habitation, and maintain them in a reasonable state of repair (s63 RTA 2010). The 2020 minimum standards require: a structurally sound dwelling, adequate natural light and ventilation, electricity, water and gas connection, working toilet, bath or shower, kitchen with cooking facilities, and window coverings in bedrooms and living rooms.

Urgent repairs (burst pipe, blocked toilet, broken hot water, dangerous electrical fault, gas leak, serious roof leak, broken fixed heater in winter) must be arranged immediately. If the landlord cannot be reached, the tenant may organise repairs up to $1,000 and be reimbursed within 14 days on production of receipts. For non-urgent repairs, tenants must give written notice and allow reasonable time.
